Frederick Engels & German Socialism as a Refuge for Radicality

Frederick Engels (1820-95) is a prominent 20th century German Socialist intellectual and a close collaborator with Engels. Engels meets Florence Kelley when she arrives for graduate studies at the University of ZÃ¼rich and immerses herself in Socialist theory and politics. Engels agrees to Kelley's English translation of Condition and writes a preface addressed particularly to the American public and working class. Kelley's translation popularizes Socialist literature and energizes the domestic American movement. Like Seligman for Ambedkar, Engels seems to function as a mentor for Kelley, who in future social work will adopt his form in Condition to present the expropriation & exploitation of women's and child labor using aggregated statistical data.
