Laurence Gronlund Brings Marx's Socialism to English-language Readers

A Danish-American lawyer, Gronlund joined the primarily German-American Socialist Labor Party, where he openly advocated for a German style trade-union based form of International Socialism. He writes Socialist pamphlets in English to popular interest in a time where Marx's and Engels' works are not yet translated from German. With Marx and Engels yet to be translated into English, Cooperative Commonwealth is sensational in America but soon disappears when English language translations such as Florence Kelley's are published. Like Ambedkar who characterizes money as “necessary to facilitate trade,” Gronlund says that “money brings [people] together; is, as it is termed, a medium of exchange between them.” Gronlund relies on Marx's labor theory of value to argue that the overproduction of Capital has an inverse relationship to wages earned: essentially, laborers can never purchase what they produce. Considering Ambedkar's monetary theory of expropriation of Indian raw materials by the British, how does Ambedkar use Socialist and particularly Marxist ideas to critique colonial monetary mismanagement?
